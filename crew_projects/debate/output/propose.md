The rapid advancement and widespread adoption of large language models (LLMs) pose significant risks that necessitate strict legal regulation. First, LLMs can generate and disseminate misinformation and harmful content at scale, influencing public opinion, elections, and social harmony. Without strict laws, there is no guarantee that developers will implement robust safeguards against such misuse. Second, LLMs can inadvertently perpetuate biases present in their training data, reinforcing discrimination and inequality. Legal frameworks are essential to enforce transparency, fairness, and accountability in their design and deployment. Third, the privacy implications are profound—LLMs often require massive datasets that may include sensitive personal information, risking data breaches and exploitation. Strict laws can mandate data protection and ethical handling standards. Finally, unregulated LLMs can threaten job security and economic stability by automating tasks without societal consent or adequate labor protections. In sum, strict laws are indispensable to ensure that the development and use of LLMs align with ethical principles, protect individual rights, and safeguard society from misuse and harm.
